Air Freight

5 Questions
Popular

Transit times vary by destination, but typical air freight from India takes:

  • Southeast Asia: 1–2 business days
  • Middle East: 1–3 business days
  • Europe: 2–4 business days
  • Americas: 3–5 business days

Express services are available for even faster delivery where required.

Air freight is billed on chargeable weight (actual weight vs volumetric weight — whichever is greater). There is no strict minimum for general air cargo. For oversized or heavy cargo, we can arrange charter solutions on request.

All air cargo must be securely packed to withstand handling and pressure changes. Common requirements include:

  • Strong outer cartons or pallets
  • Adequate cushioning for fragile items
  • Clear labelling with consignee details and weight
  • IATA compliance for dangerous goods

Yes. We have experience handling perishable goods, pharmaceuticals, and temperature-controlled cargo. These shipments are booked on suitable aircraft with temperature-controlled handling and documented cold-chain compliance.

Choose air freight when:

  • Your shipment is time-sensitive or urgent
  • The cargo is high-value with low volume/weight
  • Market demand requires immediate replenishment

Sea freight is more cost-effective for large volume, non-urgent cargo. Our team can advise the best mode based on your specific requirements.

Sea Freight

5 Questions
Popular

FCL (Full Container Load) means your cargo occupies an entire shipping container. It's ideal for large shipments and provides faster transit and higher security.

LCL (Less than Container Load) means your cargo shares space with other shippers' goods. It's cost-effective for smaller shipments but may have longer transit times due to consolidation.

Sea freight transit times from India typically range:

  • Southeast Asia: 5–12 days
  • Middle East: 7–15 days
  • Europe: 18–30 days
  • Americas: 25–40 days

Actual transit times depend on the port of loading, port of discharge, carrier, and any transshipment ports.

NVOCC stands for Non-Vessel Operating Common Carrier. We act as a carrier to our clients while booking space on actual shipping lines. This allows us to offer competitive rates, flexible scheduling, and consolidated shipments without operating our own vessels.

We primarily operate from Chennai Port and Ennore Port, with connections to other major Indian ports including Mumbai (JNPT), Mundra, Hazira, Kolkata, and Cochin. Contact us to confirm availability from your nearest port.

Yes, but dangerous goods must comply with IMDG (International Maritime Dangerous Goods) regulations. Proper classification, packing, labelling, and documentation (MSDS, DG declaration) are mandatory. Our team will guide you through the compliance process.

Land Freight

4 Questions

Our land freight services cover pan-India delivery from Chennai, with connections to all major industrial and commercial cities. We also provide cross-border transport to neighbouring countries where applicable.

We can arrange a variety of vehicle types based on your cargo requirements, including:

  • Standard container trucks (20ft / 40ft)
  • Flatbed trailers for oversized cargo
  • Refrigerated trucks for temperature-sensitive goods
  • Mini trucks and vans for smaller LTL shipments

Yes. We provide door-to-door land freight services, handling pickup from the shipper's premises and delivery directly to the consignee's location. This includes port-to-door and warehouse-to-warehouse movements.

Standard carrier liability applies to all land shipments. However, we strongly recommend taking out marine/cargo insurance for full protection against loss, damage, or theft. Our team can arrange suitable coverage at competitive rates.

Customs & Documentation

5 Questions
Popular

Yes. We provide customs brokerage and clearance services for both imports and exports at all major Indian ports and airports. Our licensed customs team ensures accurate documentation and timely release of cargo.

Common documents required for export include:

  • Commercial Invoice
  • Packing List
  • Bill of Lading or Airway Bill
  • Certificate of Origin (if required)
  • Shipping Bill (filed with Indian Customs)
  • Letter of Credit (if applicable)
  • MSDS / DG Declaration (for hazardous goods)

Requirements vary by destination country and commodity — our team will guide you through the full checklist.

Important

Import duties and taxes are the responsibility of the importer/consignee in the destination country. Under DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) terms, the exporter assumes responsibility. We recommend discussing Incoterms clearly before booking to avoid unexpected charges.

An HS (Harmonised System) Code is a standardised numerical code used globally to classify traded goods. It determines applicable duties, taxes, and trade regulations. Yes, you need the correct HS Code for every international shipment. Our customs team can assist with classification if needed.

Yes. Certain goods are prohibited or restricted from international shipment, including:

  • Illegal narcotics or controlled substances
  • Counterfeit or pirated goods
  • Certain wildlife products (CITES-restricted)
  • Unsanctioned weapons or ammunition
  • Some agricultural products without permits

Please consult our team before shipping any item you are unsure about. We will advise on compliance and feasibility.
